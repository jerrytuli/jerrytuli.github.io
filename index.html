About HopeNeli

HopeNeli Tanzania is an initiative that advocates for, supports and lobbies for women empowerment through the use of sustainable health development projects which aim to not only improve the health of consumers but also boosts the personal income of the women supported and in-turn, positively impact the national economy and social cohesiveness.

HopeNeli Tanzania presents a hope for a better tomorrow for her community. She stands for solidarity and unity in all season of life. HopeNeli Tanzania is you, HopeNeli Tanzania is us.

Why Partner with HopeNeli

HopeNeli aims to unite Tanzania in solidarity as we face and tackle challenges together. We cannot afford to sit back and expect help from outside. It is time to find alternative and sustainable ways to tackle our issues.

HopeNeli is hope for a new life. This is what we aim to give to women; hope for a new life. A partnership with HopeNeli speaks volumes not just to the women but the community at large.

HopeNeli plans to engage in spirulina production through the establishment of a pilot project in the Dar es Salaam region. The pilot project will include an array of culture ponds stirred by electrical drive paddle wheels along with some harvesting, drying and laboratory equipment.

Along with a pilot plant a demonstration unit will also be installed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the designed process and to train the locals to this relatively new activity.

HopeNeli therefore offers opportunities not just to women but creates opportunities of investments for future endeavors.

Spirulina is multicellular and filamentous blue-green microalgae that grows naturally in the alkaline waters of lakes, ponds, and rivers in warm regions. It turns sunlight into micronutrient life energy and produces about 60% of the Earth’s oxygen alone.

Spirulina has amazing properties and in many ways can be considered a Super Food. It contains the most remarkable concentration of nutrients known in any food, plant, grain, or herb. It’s composed of 60% - 70% highly digestible vegetable protein, has extremely high concentrations of beta carotene, vitamin B-12, iron and trace minerals, and the rare essential fatty acid.

The nutritional essentials in spirulina offer benefits to the human body such as mental clarity, assisting in cancer recovery, depression and so forth.

With the steady increase of the world population, unpredictable climate dynamics intensified by rampant poverty due to global economic recessions, it is unquestionably becoming apparent that the current agricultural food production systems cannot sustainably feed the world populations.

Protein food shortage in particular has remained the greatest challenge due to the irreplaceable nature of its essential amino acids leading to widespread malnutrition related complications among young children and women.

It therefore up to us to explore more protein alternative sources and spirulina microbial proteins holds out the promise of new source of protein that is independent of recognized agricultural practices. Unlike proteins from agricultural sectors, spirulina is characterized by high protein content, fast growth, less land and water requirements. Spirulina proves to be the best due to its low nucleic acid content high concentration of protein, vitamins as well as the presence of a more easily digestible cell wall.

Several scientific studies have shown spirulina to have the ability to inhibit viral replication; in particular, it was found that 5-10 mg/ml of spirulina will inhibit the HIV-1 virus otherwise known as the AIDS virus. Therefore, spirulina is effective and efficient in delaying HIV disease progression by improving the immune response.

It has also been shown to have antineoplastic and anti-inflammatory properties During the 16th session of the UN general assembly, second committee agenda item 52, a revised draft resolution on the “use of spirulina to combat hunger and malnutrition and help achieve sustainable development” was submitted by Burundi, Cameroon and Dominican Republic of Nicaragua Paraguay.
